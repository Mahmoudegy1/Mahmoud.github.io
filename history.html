History

Ancient Egypt (c. 3100 BCE - 30 BCE)

Early Dynastic Period: Around 3100 BCE, Egypt was unified under King Menes, marking the beginning of the Early Dynastic Period.

Old Kingdom: Known for the construction of the Pyramids at Giza, including the Great Pyramid of Khufu (Cheops).

First Intermediate Period: Periods of political and social upheaval.

Middle Kingdom: Characterized by stability, cultural achievements, and territorial expansion.

Second Intermediate Period: Invasions by the Hyksos, a foreign people.

New Kingdom (c. 1550 BCE - 1070 BCE)

18th Dynasty: The reign of powerful pharaohs like Hatshepsut, Thutmose III, and Akhenaten.

Amarna Period: Akhenaten's reign, known for the introduction of monotheism and the worship of Aten.

19th Dynasty: Including Ramses II, who fought the Battle of Kadesh against the Hittites.

Late Period (c. 664 BCE - 332 BCE)

Assyrian and Persian Rule: Egypt fell under the control of the Assyrians and later the Persians.

Brief Independence: During the 26th Dynasty, Egypt briefly gained independence.

Ptolemaic Period (332 BCE - 30 BCE)

Rule of the Ptolemies: Egypt became a Hellenistic kingdom under the Ptolemaic dynasty after the conquest by Alexander the Great.

Cleopatra VII: The famous queen who aligned herself with Roman leaders, including Julius Caesar and Mark Antony.

Roman Period (30 BCE - 395 CE)

Roman Conquest: Egypt became a Roman province after the defeat of Cleopatra and Mark Antony by Octavian (later Augustus).

Coptic Christianity: The emergence of Coptic Christianity in Egypt.

Byzantine Period (395 CE - 641 CE)

Eastern Roman Empire: Egypt was part of the Byzantine Empire after the Roman Empire split.

Islamic Conquest (641 CE)

Arab Conquest: Egypt came under Islamic rule after the Arab conquest led by Amr ibn al-As.

Medieval and Ottoman Periods (641 CE - 19th Century)

Mamluk Sultanate: Mamluks ruled Egypt during this period.

Ottoman Rule: Egypt became part of the Ottoman Empire.

Modern Period (19th Century - Present)

Muhammad Ali Dynasty: Muhammad Ali Pasha established a semi-autonomous state under Ottoman suzerainty.

British Occupation: Egypt came under British influence in the late 19th century.

Independence: Egypt gained full independence in 1952, and the Republic of Egypt was established.
